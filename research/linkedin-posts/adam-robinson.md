## Post 1 - AI sales reps and the future of B2B sales

Last week I took a demo with an AI sales rep.
It joined a live call, shared its screen, and walked me through real software in real time.
If you're selling commoditized SaaS, this is the future of B2B sales.
The problem with how B2B is sold today:
prospect fills a form → waits for SDR → proves budget → maybe a WEEK later sees the software.
By then? They've already moved on.
AI Demo Agents replace that entire chain.
They join live calls and guide prospects through your real product the moment they show up.
Speed is the only real moat left in commoditized SaaS.

## Post 2 - The SaaS existential crisis

I don't want to do this anymore.
Claude Code can build a full TypeForm clone in 4 hours from just a domain name.
3 problems I see:
1. Everyone is going to build everything to a degree we can't even imagine
2. That includes every SaaS scrambling to build agents that do things
3. Cutting-edge agents likely won't have SaaS margins
Without unique data or network effects, we are all screwed.

## Post 3 - SEO strategy in 2026

AI can write a 2,000-word SEO article in 10 seconds.
Google's AI Overviews are eating your clicks.
Organic traffic is down ~40% across B2B in the last year.
If your content strategy is "publish keyword-targeted blog posts and wait for Google"...
you're in trouble.
Key shifts:
- Search volume is a lie — audit for AI-resistant traffic
- Structure content to become the cited source in AI summaries
- 80% of B2B blogs are being ignored by both humans and bots
- Music, video, and human personality are the only moats left
