## Post 1 - Salesforce marketing strategy

Salesforce is worth +$200B today. It's the #1 CRM in the world.
It didn't win by having the best product. It won by dominating attention.
Marc Benioff's strategies:
- Organized fake protests at competitor events
- Hijacked all airport taxis in Nice during a competitor's event
- Made competitors part of his own story
The lesson: If you're changing the rules of the game,
doing "discreet" marketing is the biggest risk.
Attention is not requested. It is provoked.

## Post 2 - 8 LinkedIn mistakes

I got 23K followers on LinkedIn in 4 months. But I was making these mistakes:
1. Not optimizing my profile to convert
2. Posting without consistency
3. Not engaging enough with others
4. Ignoring what was already working
5. Talking about too many topics without focus
What I do now: optimized profile, post 4-5 times/week,
30 min engaging before and after each post,
only cover 3 topics within my niche.
Stop trying to seem interesting and start being useful.

## Post 3 - SaaS and AI agents

Software companies have a brutal advantage right now: data.
That's the starting point for building agent-based systems.
The SaaS that doesn't make this move will be replaced.
We're moving from a world of tabs and clicks
to a world of conversations, voice and agents executing for us.
Companies that evolve toward this have the advantage.
Those that don't will be replaced by a new generation.
