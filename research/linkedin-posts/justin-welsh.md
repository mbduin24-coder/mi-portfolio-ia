## Post 1 - Mindset and momentum

People think their environment shapes them.
I think you've already decided your life.
And now? You're just out to prove it.
Winners will go find other winners. Momentum swings up.
Losers will go find other losers. Momentum crashes down.
Both of these groups will spend their entire careers collecting evidence for the story they have already chosen.
The story they likely told themselves day one about who they are.
My newsletter tomorrow is about the two tables I watched form at my first startup.
And what happened to the two people who each sat at a different one.
One became a CEO. One basically disappeared.
Same floor. Same quota. Same product. Different decisions.

## Post 2 - Distribution as a B2B asset

You only capture about 25% of the value you create at work.
That's been the business formula forever.
You're a salesperson with a quota of $1M dollars per year.
What's your take-home pay on hitting that? Probably ~$250K.
The other $750K is reinvested into the business.
How do you capture that extra $750K?
You need to own the assets the company currently gives you:
- A product or service
- A distribution channel
I can singlehandedly create 100x more impressions for my business on social media than a typical startup.
Once you create distribution? Talk to people. Understand the problem. Build. Sell.
You can now capture 100% of the value you create.

## Post 3 - Simplifying B2B business

You're overcomplicating business.
You don't need some monster business plan to be successful.
1. Pick one problem you can solve.
2. Find one person who has it.
3. Charge them to fix it.
You don't need an LLC. You don't need a logo. You don't need a strategy doc.
You only need one person to say: Yes, I'll pay you for that.
Everything else comes after that moment. Not before.
Step 1 takes an afternoon. Step 2 takes a few DMs. Step 3 takes one conversation.
You could have a paying customer by this weekend.
