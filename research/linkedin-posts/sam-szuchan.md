## Post 1 - Trust migrated, not evaporated

Trust in mass media just hit 28%, the lowest in half a century.
Trust didn't evaporate. It migrated into rooms most people don't know exist.
Key concept: narrative infrastructure.
Power belongs to whoever controls what people think with, not what they think about.
Today's influence is moving toward 15-person Discords and Substack clusters
building the vocabulary everyone else will borrow.
If you're not in those rooms while the vocabulary is still wet,
you'll spend the next decade thinking with tools someone else designed.

## Post 2 - Vietnam and narrative frames

Samsung has poured $23.2 billion into Vietnam. Apple assembles there. Intel runs its largest chip testing facility there.
For decades, "Vietnam" activated one association in Western minds: war.
The actual country had transformed since 1986 but the frame blocked it.
Vietnam didn't argue against the old frame. It made a new one unavoidable.
Trade agreements, industrial zones, diplomatic ties — each was a cognitive permission slip.
You cannot argue your way out of a bad frame.
The frame that wins is never the most true. It's the one that reduces cognitive load.

## Post 3 - BYD vs Tesla narrative gap

BYD outsells Tesla, outspends on R&D, manufactures its own batteries and semiconductors.
29% revenue growth vs Tesla's 1%. Market cap is one-tenth of Tesla's.
The gap has almost nothing to do with cars. Everything to do with narrative infrastructure.
Three mechanisms keep Tesla's frame locked:
1. Language gates capital - Tesla's story moves in English, BYD's in Chinese
2. Celebrity compounds - Musk has 200M+ followers, Wang Chuanfu is unknown in the West
3. Price validates itself - narrative attracted buyers, buyers validated narrative
Narrative infrastructure is a valuation input — possibly the biggest.
