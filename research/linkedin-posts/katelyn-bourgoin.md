## Post 1 - How to build a $1M+ business

How to build a $1M+ business as a solopreneur (without a trustfund or getting lucky).
1. Wake up early
2. Create something valuable - solve a real problem for real people
3. Have a massive chip on your shoulder - use it as fuel
4. Build an audience (it really is worth it)
The goalposts keep moving when you're running away from something
instead of toward it. Build not to prove anything,
but because you're on a mission to help others make a bigger impact.

## Post 2 - 8 tiers of proof

Not all proof is created equal. Here's how to rank yours:
Tier 1: Borrowed proof (industry stats)
Tier 2: Self-reported results
Tier 3: Paid media mentions
Tier 4: Solicited testimonials
Tier 5: Earned third-party recognition
Tier 6: Unsolicited customer praise
Tier 7: Demonstrated results
Tier 8: Endorsement from a highly trusted source
Most experts stick to Tiers 1 and 2.
The higher tiers are where clients actually convert.

## Post 3 - Focus on what won't change

Don't ask: "How can I survive all this change?"
Ask: "What won't change?"
Jeff Bezos built Amazon by asking that exact question.
He built around things customers would always want:
lower prices, more selection, faster delivery.
People still want to buy from people they trust. Not just faceless companies.
That's not going away. Ever.
The entrepreneurs who win won't be the ones who kept up with every new tool.
They'll be the ones who knew where to focus and what to ignore.
